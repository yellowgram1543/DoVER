Design System Strategy: The Sovereign Archive1. Overview & Creative North StarCreative North Star: The Sovereign ArchiveThis design system departs from the "disposable" feel of modern SaaS and moves toward the permanence of a private legal institution. The aesthetic is "High-End Editorial"—think of a digital vault that feels as heavy and secure as a physical mahogany-and-steel safe.We break the standard grid-based "template" look through intentional asymmetry and tonal depth. By utilizing oversized serif typography against deep, obsidian-like navy surfaces, we create a sense of quiet authority. This isn't just an interface; it is a curated experience of security. We favor breathing room over density, and tonal transitions over harsh lines.---2. ColorsOur palette is anchored in the depth of the night (`surface` #0b1229) and the prestige of refined metal (`primary-container` #D4AF37).The "No-Line" Rule: To maintain a premium feel, 1px solid borders for sectioning are strictly prohibited. Boundaries must be defined solely through background color shifts. For instance, a `surface-container-low` section should sit on a `surface` background to create a natural, soft-edge boundary.Surface Hierarchy & Nesting: Treat the UI as physical layers.Base: `surface` (#0b1229)Recessed Elements: `surface-container-lowest`Elevated Components: `surface-container-highest`Use this hierarchy to nest content. A document preview should feel like it is "set into" the vault, not just floating on top.The "Glass & Gradient" Rule: Floating panels or navigation should utilize a "Glassmorphism" effect. Use `surface-bright` at 60% opacity with a 20px backdrop blur.Signature Textures: For primary actions (CTAs), do not use flat fills. Use a subtle linear gradient from `primary` (#f2ca50) to `primary-container` (#D4AF37) at a 135-degree angle. This adds a "metallic" soul to the interface.---3. TypographyThe typography is a dialogue between the old world and the new.Display & Headlines (Noto Serif): These are your "Statement" pieces. Use `display-lg` for hero moments to evoke the feeling of a prestige legal masthead. The serif nature conveys history, truth, and authority.Title, Body & Labels (Manrope): A clean, geometric sans-serif that provides a technical counter-balance. It feels modern, legible, and highly functional.The Editorial Scale: Utilize extreme contrast. Pair a `display-md` headline with a `label-md` sub-header. The gap in scale creates a sophisticated, magazine-style layout that feels custom-built for the content.---4. Elevation & DepthIn this system, depth is a function of light and material, not just shadow.The Layering Principle: Avoid the "flat web" look. Stack `surface-container` tiers to create hierarchy. A card should be `surface-container-high` placed on a `surface-container-low` page background. This creates a "soft lift."Ambient Shadows: For high-priority floating elements (like a secure document modal), use extra-diffused shadows.Value: 0px 24px 48pxColor: Use the `on-surface` color (#dce1ff) at 5% opacity. This mimics how light interacts with dark surfaces, avoiding the "dirty" look of grey shadows.The "Ghost Border" Fallback: When structural separation is required for accessibility, use a "Ghost Border." Use the `outline-variant` token at 15% opacity. This provides a whisper of a container without breaking the "No-Line" rule.Glassmorphism: Use `surface-variant` with a 0.7 alpha and 12px blur for headers. This allows the gold accents and navy backgrounds to bleed through softly as the user scrolls.---5. ComponentsButtonsPrimary: A gradient fill from `primary` to `primary-container`. Typography should be `label-md` in `on-primary` (#3c2f00) with a semi-bold weight. Use `md` (0.375rem) corner radius.Secondary: An "Outlined" feel but using the Ghost Border. Text in `primary`.Tertiary: Pure text in `secondary` with an icon. No background.Input FieldsStyling: Use `surface-container-highest` for the field background. No bottom line.States: On focus, the background remains, but a 1px "Ghost Border" using `primary` at 40% appears. Labels should use `title-sm` for a professional, form-like feel.Cards & ListsStrict Rule: No dividers. Separate list items using 12px of vertical white space or by alternating background tones between `surface-container-low` and `surface-container-lowest`.Interaction: On hover, a card should transition its background to `surface-bright` and gain an Ambient Shadow.Document Vault Components (Custom)Seal of Authenticity: A small, circular chip using `secondary-container` with an icon to denote verified documents.Timeline Tracker: Vertical lines should be replaced by a tonal "groove" using `surface-container-highest` width 4px.---6. Do's and Don'tsDo:Do embrace negative space. If a layout feels crowded, increase the padding to the next step on the scale.Do use `primary` (#f2ca50) sparingly. It is a spotlight, not a floodlight.Do use asymmetrical layouts. Align a heading to the far left and the body text to a central column to create an "editorial" rhythm.Don't:Don't use 100% white. Our brightest neutral is `on-surface` (#dce1ff). Pure white will break the dark-room immersion.Don't use `none` or `full` roundedness for standard components. Stick to `md` (0.375rem) or `lg` (0.5rem) to maintain a balance of "approachable" and "structured."Don't use standard "Success" greens unless required by accessibility. Use gold or subtle tonal shifts to indicate completion to maintain the brand's sophisticated palette.
